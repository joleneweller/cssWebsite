Where I ride

The Local Trails

For as small as we are, we have an impressive range of MTB Trails, provided the walkers stop messing about.
The biggest we have is Rostrevor, located half a mile outside Rostrevor Village, the drive for the trails is worth it every time. The two official trails worth riding (forget blue trails, start at red), is a red trail that is 27km long and the black trail at 19km long.
And yes you have to cycle uphill for a lot of that.

Barnett Demense, Castlewellan, Tardree Forest and Davagh Forest are all honourable mentions for our top stop. But this is my webpage and I make the rules so the top stop goes to 'The GAP', in Dublin.
The GAP has the most incredible trails I have ever ridden, deep berms, tabletops, rock gardens, you name it.

Training & Equipment

Training

To safely work on bicycles of any nature, it is recommended you get Cytech (Click Here!) Level 2 trained. Cytech Training consists of "intensive training and assessments" for the following topics.

  • Health & Safety in the Workshop
  • Headsets, Gears, Rim Brakes, Hubs and Bearings
  • Introduction to Disc Brakes and Hydraulic Bleeding
  • Internal Hub Gears
  • Wheel Truing & Spoke Replacement
  • Wheel Building

Equipment

Once you have completed training it's time to start working on bikes. Many brands out there are over priced for the quality of tools you recieve. It's best to start cheap, break the tools during use and then replace with a more expensive brand. The rule of thumb is the sooner it breaks the more money you spend on it.

Lets show you the more expensive side of the tooling industry. Park Tool tools at Wiggle (Click Here!) is a good example of the typical prices of such tools and equipment. Some of the tools you see you mau only use a handful of time in your entire cycle repair career, but its better to look at it and not need it, than need it and not see it.
